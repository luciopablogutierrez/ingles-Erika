üìñ Modal Verbs: Expresando Actitudes y Posibilidades

üîç What are Modal Verbs?

Modal verbs express the speaker's attitudes towards the action: possibility, necessity, ability, permission, obligation, etc.

Estructura: Subject + Modal + Base Verb + Complement

üí™ Habilidad y Capacidad

CAN / COULD

  • Can: Present ability
    "I can speak three languages."
  • Could: Past ability
    "When I was young, I could run very fast."
  • Could: Habilidad hipot√©tica
    "I could help you if I had time."

  • More formal alternative to "can"
  • "She will be able to join us tomorrow."
  • "I haven't been able to contact him."

ü§î Posibilidad y Probabilidad

MAY / MIGHT / COULD

  • May: 50% possibility
    "It may rain this afternoon."
  • Might: Less probable than "may"
    "She might come to the party."
  • Could: Theoretical possibility
    "This could be the solution."

MUST / CAN'T

  • Must: Logical deduction (95% certain)
    "He must be tired after working all day."
  • Can't: Imposibilidad l√≥gica
    "She can't be at home; I just saw her downtown."

üôè Permiso y Peticiones

CAN / MAY / COULD

  • Can: Informal
    "Can I use your phone?"
  • May: Formal
    "May I interrupt for a moment?"
  • Could: Very polite
    "Could you help me with this?"

WOULD

  • Very polite requests
  • "Would you mind opening the window?"
  • "Would you like some coffee?"

‚è∞ Modal Verbs in Past

Estructura: Modal + have + Past Participle

MUST HAVE (past deduction)

"She must have left early." (I'm sure she left early)

MIGHT/MAY HAVE (past possibility)

"He might have forgotten about the meeting." (It's possible he forgot)

COULD HAVE (unrealized ability)

"I could have helped you." (I could have helped you, but I didn't)

SHOULD HAVE (advice not followed)

"You should have called me." (You should have called me, but you didn't)

üí¨ Dialogue: In a Work Meeting

Manager: We need to discuss the project deadline. It might be too ambitious.

Sarah: I think we could finish on time if we work overtime this week.

Manager: You shouldn't have to work overtime. We should hire more staff.

Tom: May I suggest something? We could outsource some tasks.

Sarah: That might work, but it would increase our budget.

Manager: You must be right. Let me think about it. We can't exceed our budget.

Tom: Would it be possible to extend the deadline by a week?

Manager: I will have to check with the client, but they might agree.

üí° Tips to Remember

üéØ Degrees of Certainty

  • 100%: will, won't
  • 95%: must, can't
  • 50%: may, might, could
  • 0%: can't, won't

üó£Ô∏è Levels of Formality

  • Informal: can, will
  • Neutral: could, would
  • Formal: may, might
  • Very formal: ought to

üìù Context of Use

  • Peticiones: could, would, may
