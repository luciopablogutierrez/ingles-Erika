Conditionals and Future Tenses

🎯 ¿Por qué son importantes?

Los condicionales y el futuro te permiten hablar sobre posibilidades, hacer planes, predicciones y expresar situaciones hipotéticas. Son fundamentales para comunicarte sobre el futuro y las consecuencias de las acciones.

🔮 Formas del Futuro

📅 Will + Infinitive

🔧 Formación: WILL + VERB (infinitive)

✅ Afirmativo

  • I will travel (Viajaré)
  • You will study (Estudiarás)
  • He/She will work (Trabajará)
  • We will meet (Nos encontraremos)
  • They will arrive (Llegarán)

❌ Negativo

  • I won't travel (No viajaré)
  • You won't study (No estudiarás)
  • He/She won't work (No trabajará)
  • We won't meet (No nos encontraremos)
  • They won't arrive (No llegarán)

❓ Interrogativo

  • Will I travel? (¿Viajaré?)
  • Will you study? (¿Estudiarás?)
  • Will he/she work? (¿Trabajará?)
  • Will we meet? (¿Nos encontraremos?)
  • Will they arrive? (¿Llegarán?)

🎯 Usos de WILL:

🔮 Predicciones
  • "It will rain tomorrow."
  • "She will be a great doctor."
  • "Technology will change our lives."
⚡ Decisiones espontáneas
  • "I will help you with that."
  • "We will take a taxi."
  • "I will call you later."
🤝 Promesas y ofertas
  • "I will be there on time."
  • "We will not forget you."
  • "I will give you my answer tomorrow."

📋 Going to + Infinitive

🔧 Formación: BE + GOING TO + VERB

✅ Afirmativo

  • I am going to travel (Voy a viajar)
  • You are going to study (Vas a estudiar)
  • He/She is going to work (Va a trabajar)
  • We are going to meet (Vamos a encontrarnos)
  • They are going to arrive (Van a llegar)

❌ Negativo

  • I'm not going to travel (No voy a viajar)
  • You aren't going to study (No vas a estudiar)
  • He/She isn't going to work (No va a trabajar)
  • We aren't going to meet (No vamos a encontrarnos)
  • They aren't going to arrive (No van a llegar)

❓ Interrogativo

  • Am I going to travel? (¿Voy a viajar?)
  • Are you going to study? (¿Vas a estudiar?)
  • Is he/she going to work? (¿Va a trabajar?)
  • Are we going to meet? (¿Vamos a encontrarnos?)
  • Are they going to arrive? (¿Van a llegar?)

🎯 Usos de GOING TO:

📅 Planes definidos
  • "I'm going to visit my parents this weekend."
  • "We're going to move to a new house."
  • "She's going to start university next year."
🔍 Predicciones con evidencia
  • "Look at those clouds! It's going to rain."
  • "He's driving too fast. He's going to crash."
  • "She looks tired. She's going to fall asleep."
🎯 Intenciones
  • "I'm going to learn French."
  • "We're going to save money for vacation."
  • "They're going to open a restaurant."

⏰ Present Continuous para Futuro

🔧 Formación: BE + VERB-ING

🎯 Uso: Planes organizados y citas

  • "I'm meeting John at 3 PM." (Tengo una cita con John a las 3)
  • "We're flying to London tomorrow." (Tenemos vuelo a Londres mañana)
  • "She's starting her new job on Monday." (Empieza su nuevo trabajo el lunes)
  • "They're getting married next month." (Se casan el próximo mes)

🎯 Ejercicio 1: Elige la forma correcta del futuro

Selecciona will, going to, o present continuous:

1. Look at those dark clouds! It _____ rain.

2. I _____ help you with your homework.

3. We _____ dinner with friends tonight. (reservación hecha)

4. I think technology _____ change our lives completely.

🔀 Primer Condicional (First Conditional)

🔧 Estructura del First Conditional

📋 Fórmula: IF + PRESENT SIMPLE, WILL + INFINITIVE

🎯 Uso: Situaciones reales y posibles en el futuro

✅ Estructura Básica:

If + subject + present simple, subject + will + infinitive

  • "If it rains, I will stay home."
  • "If you study hard, you will pass the exam."
  • "If we leave now, we will arrive on time."
🔄 Orden Invertido:

Subject + will + infinitive + if + subject + present simple

  • "I will stay home if it rains."
  • "You will pass the exam if you study hard."
  • "We will arrive on time if we leave now."

🎭 Variaciones del First Conditional:

🤝 Con modales
  • "If you finish early, you can go home."
  • "If it's sunny, we might go to the beach."
  • "If you're late, you should call me."
📢 Con imperativos
  • "If you see John, tell him to call me."
  • "If it rains, take an umbrella."
  • "If you're hungry, make a sandwich."
❌ Forma negativa
  • "If you don't study, you won't pass."
  • "If it doesn't rain, we will go out."
  • "If she doesn't come, we will start without her."

🌟 Ejemplos Prácticos del First Conditional

🏠 Situaciones cotidianas:

🌤️ Clima
  • "If it's sunny tomorrow, we'll go to the park."
  • "If it snows, the schools will close."
  • "If the weather is nice, I'll walk to work."
💼 Trabajo
  • "If I finish this project, I'll get a promotion."
  • "If you arrive late, the boss will be angry."
  • "If we work overtime, we'll earn extra money."
🎓 Estudios
  • "If you practice every day, you'll improve quickly."
  • "If I pass this exam, I'll graduate next year."
  • "If you don't understand, ask the teacher."

🧠 Quiz: First Conditional

"If it _____ tomorrow, we _____ the picnic."
  • rains / will cancel
  • will rain / cancel
  • rain / will cancel
  • rains / cancel

🔄 Zero Conditional

🔧 Estructura del Zero Conditional

📋 Fórmula: IF + PRESENT SIMPLE, PRESENT SIMPLE

🎯 Uso: Hechos generales, verdades científicas, hábitos

🔬 Verdades científicas:
  • "If you heat water to 100°C, it boils."
  • "If you mix red and blue, you get purple."
  • "If plants don't get water, they die."
🔄 Hábitos y rutinas:
  • "If I wake up late, I feel tired all day."
  • "If she drinks coffee at night, she can't sleep."
  • "If we don't exercise, we feel lazy."
⚙️ Instrucciones y reglas:
  • "If you press this button, the machine starts."
  • "If students are late, they can't enter the classroom."
  • "If you don't have a ticket, you can't board the plane."

🔮 Expresiones de Tiempo Futuro

📅 Expresiones comunes:

⏰ Tiempo específico

  • tomorrow (mañana)
  • next week/month/year
  • in two hours (en dos horas)
  • at 3 o'clock (a las 3)
  • on Monday (el lunes)

📈 Tiempo aproximado

  • soon (pronto)
  • later (más tarde)
  • in the future (en el futuro)
  • someday (algún día)
  • eventually (eventualmente)

🔄 Secuencia temporal

  • after (después)
  • before (antes)
  • when (cuando)
  • as soon as (tan pronto como)
  • until (hasta)

🗣️ Diálogos Prácticos

🌴 Planificando vacaciones

🗣️ Diálogo:

A: What are you going to do this summer?

B: I'm going to visit my family in Spain. What about you?

A: I'm not sure yet. If I save enough money, I'll go to Japan.

B: That sounds amazing! When will you decide?

A: I'll know next month. If my boss gives me a raise, I'll definitely go.

B: I hope it works out! If you go, you'll have an incredible time.

🔍 Análisis:

  • "going to visit" → Plan definido
  • "If I save... I'll go" → First conditional (posibilidad real)
  • "will you decide" → Pregunta sobre el futuro
  • "If my boss gives... I'll go" → Condición para el futuro

🌦️ Hablando del clima

🗣️ Diálogo:

A: Look at those dark clouds! It's going to rain.

B: You're right. If it rains, we won't be able to have the barbecue.

A: What will we do instead?

B: If the weather is bad, we'll move the party inside.

A: Good idea. I'll call everyone and let them know.

B: Thanks! If you call them now, they'll have time to prepare.

⚠️ Errores Comunes

❌ Evita estos errores:

🚫 Will en la cláusula IF:

❌ Incorrecto: "If it will rain, I will stay home"

✅ Correcto: "If it rains, I will stay home"

Nunca uses will después de if en first conditional

🚫 Confundir going to y will:

❌ Incorrecto: "I will visit my parents" (plan ya hecho)

✅ Correcto: "I'm going to visit my parents"

Going to para planes, will para decisiones espontáneas

🚫 Mezclar condicionales:

❌ Incorrecto: "If you heat water, it will boil"

✅ Correcto: "If you heat water, it boils"

Zero conditional para hechos generales

🚫 Olvidar la coma:

❌ Incorrecto: "If it rains I will stay home"

✅ Correcto: "If it rains, I will stay home"

Siempre usa coma cuando if va primero

💡 Consejos para Recordar

🎯 Estrategias útiles:

  • Will vs Going to: Will = espontáneo/predicción, Going to = plan/evidencia
  • First conditional: Situaciones reales posibles (If + present, will + infinitive)
  • Zero conditional: Hechos siempre verdaderos (If + present, present)
  • Present continuous: Para citas y planes organizados
  • Nunca will después de if: En first conditional, usa present simple después de if
