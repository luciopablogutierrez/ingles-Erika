Present Perfect vs Simple Past

🎯 ¿Por qué es importante esta diferencia?

El Presente Perfecto y el Pasado Simple expresan diferentes relaciones con el tiempo. Dominar esta distinción te permitirá comunicar con precisión cuándo y cómo ocurrieron las acciones.

🕐 Presente Perfecto (Present Perfect)

🔧 Formación del Presente Perfecto

📋 Estructura: HAVE/HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE

✅ Afirmativo

  • I have worked (He trabajado)
  • You have studied (Has estudiado)
  • He/She has traveled (Ha viajado)
  • We have lived (Hemos vivido)
  • They have finished (Han terminado)

❌ Negativo

  • I haven't worked (No he trabajado)
  • You haven't studied (No has estudiado)
  • He/She hasn't traveled (No ha viajado)
  • We haven't lived (No hemos vivido)
  • They haven't finished (No han terminado)

❓ Interrogativo

  • Have I worked? (¿He trabajado?)
  • Have you studied? (¿Has estudiado?)
  • Has he/she traveled? (¿Ha viajado?)
  • Have we lived? (¿Hemos vivido?)
  • Have they finished? (¿Han terminado?)

📚 Participios Pasados (Past Participles)

🔄 Verbos Regulares: Infinitivo + -ED

work → worked
trabajar → trabajado
study → studied
estudiar → estudiado
live → lived
vivir → vivido
finish → finished
terminar → terminado

⚡ Verbos Irregulares Comunes:

go → gone
ir → ido
see → seen
ver → visto
eat → eaten
comer → comido
write → written
escribir → escrito
take → taken
tomar → tomado
make → made
hacer → hecho

🎯 Usos del Presente Perfecto

1️⃣ Experiencias de vida

Sin tiempo específico

  • "I have visited Paris." (He visitado París)
  • "She has never eaten sushi." (Nunca ha comido sushi)
  • "Have you ever been to Japan?" (¿Has estado alguna vez en Japón?)

2️⃣ Acciones recientes

Con just, already, yet

  • "I have just finished my homework." (Acabo de terminar)
  • "She has already left." (Ya se fue)
  • "Haven't you finished yet?" (¿Aún no has terminado?)

3️⃣ Acciones que continúan

Con for/since

  • "I have lived here for 5 years." (Vivo aquí desde hace 5 años)
  • "She has worked there since 2020." (Trabaja ahí desde 2020)
  • "We have been friends since childhood." (Somos amigos desde la infancia)

4️⃣ Cambios y resultados

Enfoque en el resultado presente

  • "The city has changed a lot." (La ciudad ha cambiado mucho)
  • "Technology has improved our lives." (La tecnología ha mejorado nuestras vidas)
  • "I have lost my keys." (He perdido mis llaves - no las tengo ahora)

📅 Pasado Simple (Simple Past)

🔧 Formación del Pasado Simple

📋 Verbos Regulares: Infinitivo + -ED

✅ Afirmativo

  • I worked (Trabajé)
  • You studied (Estudiaste)
  • He/She traveled (Viajó)
  • We lived (Vivimos)
  • They finished (Terminaron)

❌ Negativo

  • I didn't work (No trabajé)
  • You didn't study (No estudiaste)
  • He/She didn't travel (No viajó)
  • We didn't live (No vivimos)
  • They didn't finish (No terminaron)

❓ Interrogativo

  • Did I work? (¿Trabajé?)
  • Did you study? (¿Estudiaste?)
  • Did he/she travel? (¿Viajó?)
  • Did we live? (¿Vivimos?)
  • Did they finish? (¿Terminaron?)

⚡ Verbos Irregulares en Pasado:

go → went
ir → fui/fue
see → saw
ver → vi/vio
eat → ate
comer → comí/comió
write → wrote
escribir → escribí/escribió
take → took
tomar → tomé/tomó
make → made
hacer → hice/hizo

🎯 Usos del Pasado Simple

1️⃣ Acciones específicas en el pasado

Con tiempo específico

  • "I visited Paris last year." (Visité París el año pasado)
  • "She graduated in 2020." (Se graduó en 2020)
  • "We met yesterday." (Nos conocimos ayer)

2️⃣ Secuencia de eventos

Acciones consecutivas

  • "I woke up, had breakfast, and left home."
  • "She opened the door and saw her friend."
  • "They arrived, checked in, and went to their room."

3️⃣ Hábitos pasados

Con used to o would

  • "When I was young, I played soccer every day."
  • "She lived in London for 3 years." (Ya no vive ahí)
  • "We used to visit our grandparents every summer."

4️⃣ Estados pasados

Situaciones terminadas

  • "The weather was terrible yesterday."
  • "I felt sick last week."
  • "The movie was very interesting."

⚖️ Comparación Directa

🔍 Diferencias Clave

🟢 Presente Perfecto

  • Conexión con el presente
  • Sin tiempo específico
  • Experiencias generales
  • Acciones recientes
  • Resultados actuales
📝 Ejemplos:
  • "I have been to Italy." (Experiencia)
  • "She has just arrived." (Reciente)
  • "We have lived here for 5 years." (Continúa)

🟠 Pasado Simple

  • Acción terminada
  • Con tiempo específico
  • Eventos particulares
  • Secuencia de acciones
  • Sin conexión presente
📝 Ejemplos:
  • "I went to Italy last summer." (Específico)
  • "She arrived at 3 PM." (Tiempo específico)
  • "We lived there from 2015 to 2020." (Terminado)

🕒 Expresiones de Tiempo

⏰ Con Presente Perfecto

🔄 Experiencias

  • ever (alguna vez)
  • never (nunca)
  • before (antes)
  • several times (varias veces)

⚡ Acciones recientes

  • just (acabar de)
  • already (ya)
  • yet (todavía/aún)
  • recently (recientemente)

📅 Duración

  • for (durante/por)
  • since (desde)
  • so far (hasta ahora)
  • up to now (hasta ahora)

📅 Con Pasado Simple

📆 Tiempo específico

  • yesterday (ayer)
  • last week/month/year
  • in 2020 (en 2020)
  • two days ago (hace dos días)

🕐 Momentos específicos

  • at 3 o'clock (a las 3)
  • this morning (esta mañana)
  • when I was young
  • during the meeting

⏳ Períodos terminados

  • from... to... (de... a...)
  • between... and...
  • while (mientras)
  • after (después)

🗣️ Diálogos Prácticos

✈️ Hablando de viajes

🗣️ Diálogo:

A: Have you ever been to Japan?

B: Yes, I have. I went there last summer.

A: Really? How long did you stay?

B: I stayed for two weeks. I visited Tokyo and Kyoto.

A: That sounds amazing! Have you been to any other Asian countries?

B: Yes, I've also been to Thailand and South Korea.

🔍 Análisis:

  • "Have you ever been...?" → Experiencia general (Present Perfect)
  • "I went there last summer" → Tiempo específico (Simple Past)
  • "How long did you stay?" → Evento específico (Simple Past)
  • "I've also been to..." → Experiencias de vida (Present Perfect)

💼 En el trabajo

🗣️ Diálogo:

Manager: Have you finished the report yet?

Employee: I've already completed the first part, but I haven't finished the conclusion.

Manager: When did you start working on it?

Employee: I started it yesterday morning and worked on it for 3 hours.

Manager: Good. Have you had any problems so far?

Employee: No, everything has gone smoothly.

⚠️ Errores Comunes

❌ Evita estos errores:

🚫 Mezclar tiempos:

❌ Incorrecto: "I have seen him yesterday"

✅ Correcto: "I saw him yesterday"

Yesterday requiere Simple Past

🚫 Usar Present Perfect con tiempo específico:

❌ Incorrecto: "I have visited Paris in 2019"

✅ Correcto: "I visited Paris in 2019"

Tiempo específico = Simple Past

🚫 Confundir participios:

❌ Incorrecto: "I have went there"

✅ Correcto: "I have gone there"

Go → went (past) → gone (participle)

🚫 Olvidar auxiliares:

❌ Incorrecto: "I seen that movie"

✅ Correcto: "I have seen that movie"

Present Perfect necesita have/has

💡 Consejos para Recordar

🎯 Estrategias útiles:

  • Pregúntate "¿Cuándo?": Si hay tiempo específico → Simple Past
  • Busca conexión presente: Si afecta el presente → Present Perfect
  • Memoriza expresiones: "just", "already", "yet" → Present Perfect
  • Practica participios: Estudia verbos irregulares regularmente
  • Piensa en experiencias vs eventos: Experiencia general vs evento específico
